<DIV align="center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 24pt"><FONT style="font-size:12pt"><B>LULULEMON ATHLETICA INC. RAISES GUIDANCE FOR FOURTH QUARTER OF FISCAL 2010</B></FONT>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Vancouver, Canada &#151; January&nbsp;11, 2011 </B>&#151; lululemon athletica inc. &#091;NASDAQ: LULU; TSX: LLL&#093;
today announced that the Company is raising its guidance for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2010
ending January&nbsp;30, 2011.</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">For the fourth quarter of fiscal 2010, the Company now expects diluted earnings per share to be in
the range of $0.55 to $0.57 as compared to its previous guidance range for diluted earnings per
share of $0.46 to $0.48. The Company&#146;s improved guidance reflects stronger than anticipated net
revenues for the quarter.</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The Company now expects net revenue to be in the range of $237&nbsp;million to $239&nbsp;million for the
fourth quarter of fiscal 2010. This compares to the Company&#146;s previous guidance of net revenue in
the range of $210&nbsp;million to $215&nbsp;million for the quarter, and compares to net revenue of $161
million for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2009. Comparable-store sales for the fourth quarter of
fiscal 2010 are expected to reflect a mid- to upper-twenties percentage increase on a
constant-dollar basis. This compares to the Company&#146;s previous guidance of high teens
comparable-store sales for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2010.</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Christine Day, lululemon&#146;s CEO stated: &#147;We are wrapping up another record year for lululemon, one
in which we were able to drive strong earnings growth due to exceptional results across our retail
and e-commerce channels. In 2010, we were able to strengthen our brand awareness while making key
investments for the future. We will be chasing inventory for the remainder of the fourth quarter
and into the spring season, and in the near term will be focused once again on matching supply with
demand. For fiscal 2011, our plans remain to open 20-25 stores while making key strategic
investments to support our long term growth trajectory.&#148;</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The Company has also announced that management will be presenting at the ICR XChange Conference on
Wednesday, January&nbsp;12, 2011 in Dana Point, California. See separate release for webcast
information.</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">The Company is providing this information in light of the significant expected outperformance of
its original fourth quarter guidance provided in conjunction with the announcement of the Company&#146;s
third quarter results on December&nbsp;9, 2010. Investors should not expect the Company to provide
regular interim quarterly updates of guidance or outlook information in advance of quarterly
earnings announcement dates.</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>About lululemon athletica inc.</B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">lululemon athletica (NASDAQ:LULU; TSX:LLL) is a yoga-inspired athletic apparel company that creates
components for people to live longer, healthier and more fun lives. By producing products that
help keep people active and stress free, lululemon believes that the world will be a better place.
Setting the bar in technical fabrics and functional designs, lululemon works with yogis and
athletes in local communities for continuous research and product feedback. For more information,
visit <U>www.lululemon.com</U>.</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Non-GAAP Financial Measure</B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Constant-dollar net revenue changes, which exclude the impact of changes in foreign exchange rates,
is not a United States Generally Accepted Accounting Principle (&#147;GAAP&#148;) performance measure. We
provide constant-dollar net revenue changes because we use the measure to understand the underlying
growth rate of revenue excluding the impact on a quarter-by-quarter basis of changes in foreign
exchange rates, which are not under management&#146;s direct control. We believe that disclosing net
revenue changes on a constant-dollar basis is useful to investors because it enables them to better
understand the level of growth of our business.</DIV>
