 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Item&nbsp;7.01 Regulation FD Disclosure. </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">On December 10, 2012, Dennis J. Wilson, founder and Chairman of the Board of Directors of lululemon athletica inc. (the
&#147;Company&#148;) informed the Company that he intends to establish a pre-arranged stock trading plan to sell a portion of his stock in the Company over time as part of his individual long-term strategy for asset diversification and liquidity.
Mr.&nbsp;Wilson indicated that he intends to adopt the stock trading plan in accordance with guidelines specified under Rule 10b5-1 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 and the Company&#146;s policies regarding stock transactions. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Rule 10b5-1 permits insiders to implement written, pre-arranged stock trading plans when they are not in possession of material
non-public information. These plans establish predetermined trading parameters that do not permit the person adopting the plan to exercise any subsequent influence over how, when, or whether to effect trades. Using these plans, insiders can
gradually diversify their investment portfolios, spread stock trades out over an extended period of time to reduce market impact, and avoid concerns about transactions occurring at a time when they might possess inside information. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Under the plan Mr.&nbsp;Wilson proposes to adopt, he would sell up to 5.7&nbsp;million shares of the Company&#146;s common stock over a
period of approximately 18 months. The shares sold under the plan would include&nbsp;shares of the Company&#146;s common stock issuable upon the exchange of exchangeable shares of Lulu Canadian Holding, Inc. held by Mr.&nbsp;Wilson. Lulu Canadian
Holding, Inc. is an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of the Company. Exchangeable shares of Lulu Canadian Holding, Inc. may be exchanged on a one-for-one basis for shares of the Company&#146;s common stock. If Mr.&nbsp;Wilson completed all the
planned sales under this Rule 10b5-1 trading plan, he would beneficially own approximately 36.9&nbsp;million shares (approximately 25.6%) of the Company&#146;s outstanding common stock and common stock issuable upon exchange of exchangeable shares.
</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Mr.&nbsp;Wilson indicated that the transactions under this plan would commence no earlier than January&nbsp;10, 2013 and will
be disclosed publicly through required Form 144 and Form 4 filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission. </FONT></P>
