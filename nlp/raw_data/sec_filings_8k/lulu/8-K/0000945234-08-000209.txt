<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B><I>2008 Executive Bonus Plan</I></B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On April&nbsp;30, 2008, the Compensation Committee (the &#147;Compensation Committee&#148;) of the Board of
Directors (the &#147;Board&#148;) of lululemon athletica inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) approved a 2008 Executive Bonus
Plan (the &#147;Plan&#148;) and related financial performance goals in respect of the fiscal year beginning February&nbsp;4, 2008 (&#147;Fiscal
2008&#148;). The Plan is applicable to the positions of executive vice president and above, and to other
senior officers of the Company as designated by the Compensation Committee. Target bonuses for the
Company&#146;s executive officers eligible to participate in the Plan are weighted so that 90% of the
target bonus is based on financial performance goals and 10% is based on individual performance
goals. The maximum allowable bonus payout is 120% of the target bonus amount.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The financial performance goals for each of the executive officers eligible to participate in
the Plan are based on four financial measures for Fiscal 2008: (i)&nbsp;the Company&#146;s diluted earnings
per share; (ii)&nbsp;comparable store sales; (iii)&nbsp;operating margins; and (iv)&nbsp;inventory levels (the
&#147;Financial Measures&#148;). Individual performance goals vary depending on the responsibilities of the
positions held by each Plan participant. The Fiscal 2008 target bonus amounts and weighting
between the financial and individual performance goals for each executive officer are set forth in
the table below.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The following is an example of how a maximum allowable payout of 120% of the target amount
could affect total bonus payouts for Fiscal 2008. <B><I>This example is included for illustrative
purposes only and does not represent a forecast or target by the Company for Fiscal 2008 of the
Financial Measures.</I></B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Because each executive vice president participating in the Plan has a target bonus of 60% of
base salary, and because 90% of that target bonus is allocated to achieving the financial
performance goals and 10% of that target bonus is allocated to achieving the individual performance
goals, the target bonus attributable to achievement of the financial performance goals is 54% of base
salary (<I>i.e. </I>90% x 60% = 54%) and the target bonus attributable to achievement of the individual
performance goals is 6% of base salary (<I>i.e.</I>, 10% x 60% = 6%). If the Financial Measures and
individual performance were at a level permitting a payout of 120% of the target bonus, an
executive vice president participating in the Plan would be eligible to receive 72% of his or her
base salary (64.8% for achieving the financial performance goals (120% x 54%) and 7.2% for achieving
the individual performance goals (120% x 6%)).
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B><I>Retirement, Transition and Release Agreement between Company and Robert Meers</I></B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As previously reported in the Company&#146;s Current Report on Form 8-K filed on April&nbsp;2, 2008, Robert Meers announced that he will resign from the office of Chief Executive
Officer of the Company effective June&nbsp;30, 2008. Mr.&nbsp;Meers has also determined that he will also
resign from the Company&#146;s board of directors, and from all officer and director positions he
currently holds with the Company&#146;s affiliates, effective June&nbsp;4, 2008.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mr.&nbsp;Meers and the Company have entered into a Retirement, Transition and Release Agreement
dated May&nbsp;6, 2008, under which Mr.&nbsp;Meers has agreed to consult with the Company upon the Company&#146;s
request through January&nbsp;31, 2009, and to release the Company from any claims he may have related to
his employment with the Company and any of its affiliates. The Company has also released Mr.&nbsp;Meers
from any claims related to Mr.&nbsp;Meers&#146; employment by the Company. Mr.&nbsp;Meers has also agreed to
continue to be bound by certain restrictive covenants, including a covenant not to compete with the
Company for a period of 24&nbsp;months following the termination of his employment, to preserve the
confidentiality of certain of the Company&#146;s proprietary information, and to any assignment of any
rights he may have in the Company&#146;s intellectual property to the Company. In consideration for
such consultation services, release and restrictive covenants, the Company will accelerate the
vesting of 209,042 options under non-qualified stock options currently held by Mr.&nbsp;Meers.
Consistent with applicable tax regulations, the Company has extended the exercise date for such
options to a date between January&nbsp;1, 2009 and March&nbsp;15, 2009. Mr.&nbsp;Meers will also be entitled to
receive a special bonus based on the Company&#146;s financial performance and Mr.&nbsp;Meer&#146;s performance for
Fiscal 2008 pursuant to the terms of the Plan, in an amount not to exceed CDN$219,113. A copy of
the Retirement, Transition and Release Agreement is attached hereto as Exhibit&nbsp;10.2 and is
incorporated herein by reference.
</DIV>
