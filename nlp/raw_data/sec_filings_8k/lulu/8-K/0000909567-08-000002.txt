<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Item&nbsp;5.02.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of
Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers.</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On January 4, 2008, lululemon athletica inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) announced that Christine M. Day
had been appointed to serve as the Company&#146;s Executive Vice President, Retail Operations. Ms.&nbsp;Day,
age 45, joins the Company from Starbucks Coffee International, a retailer of specialty coffee,
where she served as President, Asia Pacific Group from July&nbsp;2004 through February&nbsp;2007. From July
2003 to October&nbsp;2003, Ms.&nbsp;Day served as Co-President for Starbucks Coffee International. From
1987 to 2003, Ms.&nbsp;Day served in various capacities at Starbucks Coffee Company, including Senior
Vice President, North American Finance &#038; Administration; and
Vice President of Sales and Operations for Business Alliances. Ms.
Day is a member of the board of directors of Select Comfort Corporation, a provider of adjustable-firmness beds and other sleep-related accessory products, and Nu Skin Enterprises, Inc., a direct seller of personal skin care and nutritional products. Ms.&nbsp;Day received
a B.A. in Administrative Management from Central Washington University and is a graduate of Harvard Business School&#146;s Advanced Management Program. Ms.&nbsp;Day will join us on or about January&nbsp;7,
2008.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the terms of her offer letter (a copy of which is attached as Exhibit&nbsp;10.1 to this Form
8-K), Ms.&nbsp;Day will receive an annual base salary of CDN$365,000. Ms.&nbsp;Day is also eligible to
receive an annual performance bonus of at least 60% of her base salary for the applicable fiscal
year, if specified corporate and individual performance goals are met for that year. Pursuant to
the terms of her offer letter, the Company also intends to grant to Ms.&nbsp;Day options to purchase
125,000 shares of common stock in connection with her initial hire, options to purchase 41,667
shares of common stock on the first and second anniversary of her employment start date and options
to purchase 41,666 shares of common stock on the third anniversary of her employment start date.
All options will have a fair market value exercise price and will vest 25% per year for four years
on each anniversary of the effective grant date of the option.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ms.&nbsp;Day will also be reimbursed for up to US$80,000 of reasonable relocation expense incurred
by her, a temporary housing allowance up to CDN$1,500 per month for six months and CDN$4,500 of tax
services for the 2008 tax filing year. Ms.&nbsp;Day is also entitled to participate in the employee
benefit arrangements generally available to our employees.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In addition, Ms.&nbsp;Day will be entitled to 12&nbsp;months of base salary if her employment with the
Company is terminated without cause within her first 12&nbsp;months of employment and she will be
entitled to severance based on British Columbia, Canada employment and labor standards if her
employment is terminated without cause after 12&nbsp;months of employment with the Company, provided,
that Ms.&nbsp;Day executes an appropriate non-disparagement and non-compete agreement with the Company.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is no family relationship between Ms.&nbsp;Day and any of the executive officers or directors
of the Company. In addition, Ms.&nbsp;Day is not a party to any transaction with the Company or its
subsidiaries that would require disclosure under Item 404(a) of the Securities and Exchange
Commission Regulation&nbsp;S-K.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company&#146;s press release dated January&nbsp;4, 2008 announcing the appointment of Ms.&nbsp;Day as
described above is filed herewith as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In connection with the hiring of Ms.&nbsp;Day, the Company changed the title of its Chief Operating
Officer, Michael Tattersfield, to Executive Vice President, Retail Logistics and Sourcing, in order
to permit him to focus on supply chain and production issues as the Company continues to grow.
</DIV>

</DIV>


<DIV align="center" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 0pt"><I>Appointment of 20-Year Starbucks Veteran to lululemon&#146;s Senior Leadership Team</I>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><B>January&nbsp;4, 2007 &#151;  Vancouver, Canada </B>&#151; lululemon athletica inc. &#091;NASDAQ: LULU; TSX: LLL&#093; today
announced that Christine Day has been appointed to its Senior Leadership Team as Executive Vice
President, Retail Operations. In this newly created role, Ms.&nbsp;Day will oversee all of lululemon&#146;s
retail operations in North America and on an international basis, as well as the Company&#146;s
Community Relations, Real Estate Development, the Guest Education Center and Wholesale
businesses.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Ms.&nbsp;Day brings more than 20&nbsp;years of consumer and
retail industry experience to lululemon from
Starbucks, where she held several senior management positions since joining the company in 1986.
Ms.&nbsp;Day most recently served as President of the Asia Pacific Group of Starbucks Coffee
International from July&nbsp;2004 to February&nbsp;2007. From July&nbsp;2003 to October&nbsp;2003, she served as
Co-President for Starbucks Coffee International. From 1987 to 2003, Ms.&nbsp;Day served in various
capacities for Starbucks Coffee Company, including Senior Vice President, North American Finance
and Administration; and Vice President of Sales and Operations for Business Alliances.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Ms.&nbsp;Day has a BA from Central Washington University and is a graduate of Harvard Business
School&#146;s Advanced Management Program. Christine is currently a director of Select Comfort
Corporation, a provider of adjustable-firmness beds and other sleep-related accessory products
and Nu Skin Enterprises, Inc. a direct seller of personal skin care and nutritional products.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&#147;We
continue to develop lululemon&#146;s Senior Leadership Team,&#148; said Robert Meers, CEO of lululemon
athletica, &#147;Christine&#146;s extensive experience overseeing the retail operations of a rapidly
expanding and international company like Starbucks will be a tremendous asset to us as lululemon
grows.&#148;
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">To support the continued growth of the Company and in connection with the hiring of Ms.&nbsp;Day,
lululemon Chief Operating Officer, Mike Tattersfield, has been named Executive Vice President,
Retail Logistics and Sourcing.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Mr. Meers
continued, &#147;Mike Tattersfield has been instrumental in leading our
growth through fiscal year 2007, with lululemon successfully opening 27 new stores throughout North
America, sourcing and hiring many of our senior management team, and formalizing the &#145;lululemon
experience&#146;&#148;. &#147;This new role will permit Mike to focus
on the critical areas of supply chain
and production as the Company expands, overseeing the Production, Merchandise Management,
Planning &#038; Allocation, Logistics, and Factory Compliance and Social Responsibility teams.&#148;
</DIV>

components for people to live longer, healthier and more fun lives. By producing products that

Setting the bar in technical fabrics and functional designs, lululemon works with yogis and
athletes in local communities for continuous research and product feedback. For more information,
